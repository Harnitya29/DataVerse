üìã Key Findings & Narrative ‚Äî Insight Summary
"Development progress is multidimensional ‚Äî not purely economic."

Act I (2000‚Äì2010): The Era of Expansion. Across 265 countries, the Global Development Resilience Index rose steadily for all income groups ‚Äî high-income nations climbed from an average of 40.5 to 51.2, while low-income nations improved from 12.2 to 23.5. Economic growth was concentrated in North America and Europe, but the gap between income groups persisted, revealing that expansion was real but unequal. Cambodia emerged as a standout, growing its Economic Opportunity Index from 305 to 895 by 2010, defying its lower-middle-income classification.

Act II (2011‚Äì2019): The Pivot to Quality. A systemic tension emerged: green transition scores were highest in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia ‚Äî not by environmental policy, but by low industrialisation ‚Äî while high-income nations with moderate green scores recorded the highest economic growth. The correlation between green transition and resilience was ‚àí0.34, confirming a real trade-off. Meanwhile, digital connectivity rose globally, yet the "Progress Paradox" surfaced: Iran (digital readiness 0.93) and Morocco (0.87) showed economic opportunity scores below 700 and 910 respectively, proving that connectivity alone does not generate prosperity. Cambodia continued its rise, reaching an Economic Opportunity Index of 4,285.6 by 2019 ‚Äî a 1,303% gain from 2000.

Act III (2020): The Resilience Test. The pandemic wiped out decades of gains in a single year. Every region collapsed: Sub-Saharan Africa lost 78% of resilience, South Asia 68%, Europe & Central Asia 64%. The Human Development Index was the strongest predictor of resilience (r = 0.893), far outweighing GDP size or digital readiness (r = 0.689). Brazil ‚Äî with a GDP of $1.44 trillion ‚Äî saw its resilience score collapse from 55.0 to 10.6 (an 81% drop), exposing the fragility of scale without systemic depth. High-income nations averaged only 19.8 in resilience by 2020, down from 55.6 in 2017 ‚Äî confirming that development progress is multidimensional, and no income group was fully immune.
